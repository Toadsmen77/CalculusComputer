The development of calculus was driven by the study of the natural universe. Calculus's origin takes place in the late 1600s, where a plethora of natural philosophers(people who studied and the natural universe and used math to explain physical phenomena) were battling with problems which could not be solved with the then-current tool set provided by math. These problems include:

  • If the velocity of an object constantly changed, how could we determine the distance that the object travelled?
  • If an object is oddly shaped(curved), how could we determine its area and volume?

These problems may seem trivial to you if you already know calculus. Obviously, we can simply just use our handy-dandy differentiation rules like the chain rule and our beloved integration techniques like partial fraction decomposition, but unfortunately the calculus tool set was not readily available to natural philosophers before the late 1600s.
